Thoughts on Technlogy & Design

As a computer science graduate with a minor in visual communication, I am interested in the intersection between technology and design.

The weaving of modern tech with digital design is a relatively new concept. Considering everyday people weren't able to create and digitally design themselves using a computer until around the 1980s. This is when programming languages took off, popularizing design tools, browsers, and software languages such as Adobe, Google Chrome, Microsoft Internet Explorer, and C++. And interdependence between tech and design is seen even more in the past couple years in unique jobs popping up in mainstream culture. Jobs such as, data visializer, product designer, blockchain smart contract creator, and VR designer to name a few of my favorites. These two are inevitably connected and will continue to be.

And in my mind, what connects these two? Most often technology is created to solve a unique problem for a group of users. The success of the technology solution is likely dependent on the design. The design needs to address the users needs in form and function. We can't have useful technology without design, and more specifically human-centered design.

Technlogy & Design

As a computer science graduate with a minor in visual communication, I am interested in the intersection between technology and design.

Design as been around for ages - think 38,000 BCE cave painting, but technology (at least modern technology such as the cellphone, computer, tv, and the cloud to name a few) has only been around since the late 1920s (invention of the tv). That is roughly 100 years where these two intertwined. And even shorter if we count from when everyday people were able to create and digitally design themselves using a computer around the 1980s when programming languages took off, popularizing tools, browsers, and software languages such as Adobe, Google Chrome, Microsoft Internet Explorer, and C++.

The weaving of modern tech with digital design is a relatively new concept. And it's seen even more in the past couple years where jobs like data visializer, product designer, blockchain smart contract creator, and VR designer are popping up more in mainstream culture. Yet, as a new space these two continue to be inevitably connected.

And in my mind, what connects these two? - problem solving. Most often technology is created to solve a unique problem of a user. The success of the technology solution is likely dependent on the design. The design needs to address the users needs in form and function. We can't have useful technology without design & successful design without technology.
